Enviromental Impact

The tornado put up massive amounts of dust, debris, and pollutants, making the air quality very bad.Trees were uprooted, vegetation stripped bare, and topsoil eroded.The EF-5 tornado reshaped the local landscape, which disrupted ecosystems and reduced biodiversity.The tornado also accelerated the loss of green spaces, which had previously helped mitigate urban heat and improve air quality.With the loss of vegetation, animals were forced to relocate or adapt to new environments, often struggling to find resources. Native plant species that were wiped out by the tornado.

Ways to restore the enviroment

To mitigate the effects of soil erosion and reduce land fertility loss, various conservation practices can be implemented. One effective method is to plant cover crops, such as grasses or legumes, which can help to stabilize the soil, prevent erosion, and improve soil health.

Economic impact

7500 residential dwellings damaged by the storm; of those, well over half, 4,000, were completely destroyed, causing some 9,200 people to have no homes. Mercy Hospital Joplin was demolished, as were Joplin High School and several elementary schools. The city itself sustained damage to two fire stations, both of which were completely destroyed, as well as damage to half a dozen city parks.553 businesses had been destroyed or severely damaged by the 2011 tornado, affecting nearly 5,000 employees. Counting all the commercial and residential property affected, as well as disposal of the nearly three million cubic yards of residential debris throughout the disaster area, and demolition of condemned properties, the total cost of the Joplin tornado was just over two billion dollars.
